<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>AMC filters for the semiconductor market consist of different types of filters used to remove airborne molecular contamination. Chemisorption filters, such as activated carbon or aluminas, work by chemically bonding with the contaminants. They effectively capture volatile organic compounds and other harmful gases. On the other hand, bonded media panels are made of activated carbon compressed into a single-piece panel. These panels provide a large surface area for adsorption and are commonly used for removing organic and inorganic gases from cleanroom environments in the semiconductor industry.</p></p>
