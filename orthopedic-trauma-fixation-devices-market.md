<p><p>Orthopedic trauma fixation devices are used to stabilize and treat fractures, dislocations, and other orthopedic injuries. Metallic fixators, such as plates, screws, and intramedullary nails, are commonly used due to their strength and durability. Bioabsorbable fixators are made of materials that gradually dissolve over time, reducing the need for additional surgery to remove them. Other types of fixation devices may include external fixators and hybrid fixators, which combine metallic and bioabsorbable components. These devices play a crucial role in providing stability and promoting healing in orthopedic trauma cases.</p></p>
