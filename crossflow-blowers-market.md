<p><p>Crossflow blowers are categorized into two types based on their installation orientation: horizontal installation and vertical installation. </p><p>Horizontal installation refers to the placement of the blower horizontally, with the air outlet on the side. It is commonly used when there is limited vertical space available. This type of installation is preferred for applications such as cooling electronic devices or appliances.</p><p>In contrast, vertical installation involves mounting the blower vertically, with the air outlet on the top or bottom. This configuration is often used when there is sufficient vertical space but limited horizontal space. It is commonly used in heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC) systems or in industrial applications where efficient air circulation is required.</p></p>
