<p><p>Single-cell genome sequencing technology refers to methods used to analyze the DNA of individual cells. Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS) is a powerful technique that enables high-throughput sequencing of multiple cells simultaneously. Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) amplifies specific DNA regions from single cells for analysis. Quantitative PCR (qPCR) allows measurement of gene expression levels in individual cells. Microarrays use DNA probes to identify specific sequences in single cells. Multiple Displacement Amplification (MDA) is a method of amplifying limited DNA amounts from single cells. These technologies are used in various research fields to understand cellular heterogeneity and genetic variations.</p></p>
