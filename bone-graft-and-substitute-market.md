<p><p>Bone graft and substitute refers to a procedure used in orthopedic surgeries to replace or repair damaged bone tissue. It involves the transplantation of bone or bone-like material, known as a graft, to stimulate the growth of new bone, enhance healing, and restore the function of the affected area. Bone grafting is used in various orthopedic conditions such as fractures, orthopedic injuries, bone infections, and bone tumors.</p><p>The bone graft and substitute market is witnessing significant growth due to various factors. The rising prevalence of bone and joint disorders, such as osteoarthritis, osteoporosis, and fractures, is driving the demand for bone graft and substitute procedures. Additionally, the growing geriatric population, increasing incidences of road accidents, and sports-related injuries contribute to the market growth.</p><p>Furthermore, advancements in technology, such as the development of synthetic bone grafts and substitutes, have revolutionized the market. These alternatives provide improved biocompatibility, reduced risk of infections, and faster healing. Moreover, the availability of various bone graft materials, such as autografts, allografts, xenografts, and synthetic substitutes, allows surgeons to choose the most suitable option for each patient.</p><p>The market is also witnessing trends such as the increasing use of biocompatible synthetic bone grafts, the adoption of minimally invasive surgical techniques, and the incorporation of growth factors into graft materials to enhance bone regeneration.
