<p><p>Concentrating Solar Power (CSP) refers to a renewable energy technology that harnesses the sun's energy for electricity generation. The four main types of CSP technologies are parabolic trough, solar tower, linear Fresnel, and dish/engine systems. Parabolic trough systems use curved mirrors to focus sunlight onto a tube filled with fluid, generating steam and powering a turbine. Solar tower systems use an array of mirrors to concentrate sunlight onto a receiver atop a tower, producing steam for electricity generation. Linear Fresnel systems work similarly to parabolic troughs but use flat mirrors. Dish/engine systems involve reflecting sunlight onto a receiver at the focus of a dish-shaped reflector, powering a generator.</p></p>
