<p><p>Tissue sealants are used in medical procedures to promote healing and reduce bleeding. Fibrin sealants are made from human or animal blood and contain fibrinogen and thrombin, which work together to form a clot. Synthetic sealants, on the other hand, are created using synthetic materials and typically contain a mixture of polymers that adhere to tissues and create a seal. Both types of sealants are used in various surgical procedures to provide hemostasis and improve patient outcomes.</p></p>
