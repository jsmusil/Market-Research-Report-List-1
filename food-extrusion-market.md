<p><p>Food extrusion is a popular method used in the food industry to produce various food products. Cold extrusion refers to the process of pushing food ingredients through a cold die, resulting in products with a soft texture. On the other hand, hot extrusion involves using heat to soften the ingredients, which are then forced through a die to create products with a firm texture. Both types of extrusion play a significant role in the market, enabling the production of a wide range of food items with different textures and characteristics.</p></p>
