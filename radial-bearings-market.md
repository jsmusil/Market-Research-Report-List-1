<p><p>Radial bearings are mechanical components used to support loads and reduce friction in rotating machinery. They are primarily classified into three market types: metal, ceramic, and others. Metal bearings, typically made of steel, are widely used due to their durability and cost-effectiveness. Ceramic bearings, made from materials like silicon nitride, offer superior heat resistance and are often preferred in high-speed applications. The "other" category includes bearings made from alternative materials such as plastic or composite materials. These options are chosen for specialized conditions or unique requirements where conventional metal or ceramic bearings may not be suitable.</p></p>
