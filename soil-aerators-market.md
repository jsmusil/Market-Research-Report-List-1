<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Soil aerators are divided into two types - mechanical and pneumatic. Mechanical soil aerators use rotating blades or spikes to break up compacted soil and improve aeration. They typically have a fixed working depth and work by physically penetrating the soil. On the other hand, pneumatic soil aerators use compressed air to create pressure and inject it into the soil, fracturing compacted layers. They can work at variable depths and require minimal soil disturbance. Both types serve the purpose of enhancing soil aeration and improving overall soil health.</p></p>
