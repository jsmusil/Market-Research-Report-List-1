<p><p>Continuous Wave (CW) radar systems are used to detect the presence and position of objects by emitting a continuous radio frequency signal. Unmodulated Continuous Wave (UCW) radar is a type of CW radar that transmits a continuous, uninterrupted wave to the target and measures the return signal characteristics. On the other hand, Modulated Continuous Wave (MCW) radar uses pulses to transmit the signal, allowing for better measurement accuracy and range resolution. Both UCW and MCW radars have applications in various industries including defense, aerospace, and automotive for surveillance, navigation, and sensing purposes.</p></p>
