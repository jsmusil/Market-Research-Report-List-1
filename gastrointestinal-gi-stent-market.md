<p><p>Gastrointestinal (GI) stents are medical devices used to treat obstruction in the gastrointestinal tract, which may occur due to various conditions such as cancer, strictures, or benign diseases. These stents are typically self-expandable, made of metal or plastic, and are placed endoscopically to provide support in the narrowed or blocked areas of the GI tract, thereby improving the flow of fluids and preventing further complications.</p><p>The global gastrointestinal and GI stent market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. Factors driving the market growth include an increasing prevalence of gastrointestinal disorders, a growing aging population, advancements in stent technology, and a rise in minimally invasive procedures.</p><p>Moreover, the rising incidence of colorectal cancer, esophageal cancer, and other GI tract-related tumors is also contributing to the market growth. Additionally, the adoption of advanced stent materials with improved biocompatibility and excellent clinical outcomes is further fueling the demand for GI stents.</p><p>The market is also witnessing several trends that are shaping its growth. One prominent trend is the increasing preference for biodegradable stents, which are designed to degrade and be absorbed by the body over time, eliminating the need for stent removal procedures.</p><p>Furthermore, the development of next-generation stents with drug-eluting properties is set to revolutionize the GI stent market. These drug-eluting stents are capable of controlled drug release, which helps in preventing inflammation, reducing restenosis rates, and improving patient outcomes.</p><p>In conclusion, with a CAGR of 12.2% during the forecast period, the gastrointestinal and GI stent market is projected to experience substantial growth. Factors such as the rising prevalence of gastrointestinal disorders, advancements in stent technology, and the adoption of biodegradable and drug-eluting stents are expected to drive market growth in the coming years.</p></p>
