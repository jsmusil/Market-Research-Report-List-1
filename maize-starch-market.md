<p><p>Maize Starch, also known as corn starch, is a carbohydrate extracted from the endosperm of maize kernels. It is commonly used as a thickening agent and stabilizer in various food and industrial applications. The starch contains amylose and amylopectin, which provide the necessary properties for its application in food products such as soups, sauces, puddings, and bakery items.</p><p>The market for maize starch has been witnessing significant growth in recent years. The increasing demand for convenience foods, processed foods, and bakery products is a major factor driving the market growth. Maize starch is preferred over other starches due to its excellent thickening and binding properties, along with its neutral taste and odor. Additionally, the rising awareness about gluten-free diets has further fueled the use of maize starch as a substitute for wheat-based products in various food applications.</p><p>Furthermore, the growth of the paper industry has also contributed to the demand for maize starch as it is used in paper sizing applications. The pharmaceutical industry also utilizes maize starch in tablet manufacturing as a binder and disintegrant. The market growth is further supported by the expanding industrial applications of maize starch, such as in the production of adhesives, textiles, and bioplastics.</p><p>In terms of regional analysis, the North American and European markets have witnessed significant growth due to the high consumption of processed and convenience foods. This growth can be attributed to the increasing demand for convenience foods, gluten-free products, and industrial applications.
