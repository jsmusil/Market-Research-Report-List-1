<p><p>Cyanide ion selective electrodes are devices used to measure the concentration of cyanide ions in a solution. There are three main market types for these electrodes - crystal membrane, liquid membrane, and PVC membrane. Crystal membrane electrodes use a crystal or solid-state material to respond to the cyanide ions. Liquid membrane electrodes utilize an organic liquid that selectively interacts with the ions. PVC membrane electrodes have a polyvinyl chloride (PVC) membrane that interacts with the cyanide ions. These different market types offer a variety of options for measuring cyanide ion concentration based on the desired application.</p></p>
