<p><p>Cell-free protein synthesis (CFPS) is a method used to produce proteins in vitro without the use of living cells. It involves the extraction of cellular machinery, such as ribosomes and enzymes, from cells, which are then mixed with the necessary components for protein synthesis, including DNA templates, amino acids, and energy sources. CFPS offers a rapid and flexible method for synthesizing proteins, allowing researchers to produce complex proteins more quickly and efficiently than traditional cell-based methods.</p><p>The cell-free protein synthesis market is witnessing significant growth due to several factors. Firstly, the increasing demand for therapeutic proteins and biologics has driven the need for efficient and scalable protein production methods.
