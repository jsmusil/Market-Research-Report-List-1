<p><p>Eri Silks, also known as Endi Silks or Errandi Silks, are a type of silk fabric produced from the cocoons of the Eri silk worm (Philosamia ricini). Eri Silk production is a traditional cottage industry in these regions, with many households involved in the rearing of silk worms and the subsequent processing of silk.</p><p>The Eri Silks market has been witnessing significant growth in recent years. This growth can be attributed to several factors. Firstly, the increased consumer demand for eco-friendly and sustainable fabrics has boosted the market for Eri Silks, as it is produced without harming the silk worms. Additionally, Eri Silks' thermal insulation properties have made it popular among consumers, especially in colder regions.</p><p>Furthermore, the rise in disposable income has led to increased spending on luxury and high-quality fabrics, thus driving the demand for Eri Silks. The market growth is also fueled by the popularity of Eri Silks in the fashion and textile industries, where it is used in the production of sarees, shawls, stoles, and other garments.</p><p>According to market research, the Eri Silks market is projected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 11.6% during the forecast period.
