<p><p>A wafer testing system is a machine used to test integrated circuits (ICs) on semiconductor wafers. It is an essential step in the semiconductor manufacturing process to ensure the quality and reliability of ICs before they are packaged and shipped to customers. The wafer testing system performs electrical, functional, and parametric tests on the ICs to identify any manufacturing defects or performance issues.</p><p>The global wafer testing system market has been experiencing steady growth in recent years. The increasing demand for consumer electronics, such as smartphones, tablets, and gaming consoles, is one of the major factors driving the market growth. These devices use a large number of ICs, and the growing popularity and adoption of these devices are contributing to the demand for wafer testing systems.</p><p>The market is also witnessing the adoption of advanced technologies, such as System-on-Chip (SoC) and Internet of Things (IoT) devices, which require high-performance ICs. This is further fueling the demand for wafer testing systems that can accurately test and validate the functionality and performance of these complex ICs.</p><p>Moreover, the emergence of new applications, such as artificial intelligence (AI), autonomous vehicles, and augmented reality (AR), is also driving the market growth. <p><p>The wafer testing system market includes two types of system structures: single-layer system structure and multi-layer system structure. In a single-layer system structure, the testing components are arranged in a single layer, offering simplicity and cost-efficiency. On the other hand, the multi-layer system structure consists of multiple layers of testing components, which provides higher performance and flexibility but at a higher cost. Both types cater to different customer needs, with single-layer structures suiting simpler applications, while multi-layer structures are suitable for more complex testing requirements.</p></p>
