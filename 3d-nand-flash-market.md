<p><p>The 3D NAND Flash market consists of various types, including Multi-Level Cell (MLC), Trinary-Level Cell (TLC), and others. MLC involves storing multiple bits of data on each memory cell, enabling higher storage capacity but at a higher cost. TLC, on the other hand, allows for even greater data storage by storing three bits per cell. Both MLC and TLC are widely used and offer a balance between capacity and cost. The "Others" category encompasses emerging types, such as Quad-Level Cell (QLC), which offer even higher storage capacity but come with their own trade-offs.</p></p>
