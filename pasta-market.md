<p><p>Dry pasta and fresh pasta are two main types of pasta available in the market. Dry pasta is made by mixing durum wheat flour with water and then drying it, resulting in a long shelf life. It is commonly found in various shapes and sizes like spaghetti or macaroni. On the other hand, fresh pasta is made with soft wheat flour and eggs, giving it a delicate texture and short shelf life. It is often sold refrigerated and is preferred for its rich taste and versatility in cooking.</p></p>
