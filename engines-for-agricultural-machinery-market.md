<p><p>Engines for agricultural machinery are categorized into three main types: gasoline engines, diesel engines, and gas engines. Gasoline engines are designed to run on gasoline fuel and are commonly used in smaller machinery such as lawnmowers and chainsaws. Diesel engines run on diesel fuel and are preferred for larger machinery like tractors and combines due to their power and efficiency. Gas engines, on the other hand, can run on either gasoline or natural gas, making them adaptable and commonly used in stationary machinery or applications where natural gas is more accessible.</p></p>
