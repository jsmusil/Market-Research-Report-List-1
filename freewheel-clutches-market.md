<p><p>Freewheel clutches are mechanical devices that transmit torque in one direction while allowing free rotation in the opposite direction. There are three types of freewheel clutches: sprag type, roller type, and ball bearing type. </p><p>Sprag type clutches use sprags or small ramps and rollers to engage and disengage the clutch. Roller type clutches employ rollers between two concentric races to facilitate rotation in one direction. Ball bearing type clutches use balls between two races, providing smooth and efficient operation. Each type has its own unique design and application depending on the specific torque and speed requirements.</p></p>
