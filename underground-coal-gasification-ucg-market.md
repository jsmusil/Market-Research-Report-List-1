<p><p>Underground Coal Gasification (UCG) is a process that converts coal into valuable gas by burning it underground. The market types for UCG include steam reforming, partial oxidation, auto-thermal reforming, combined or two-step reforming, and biomass gasification. </p><p>Steam reforming involves reacting coal with steam to produce hydrogen gas, while partial oxidation involves a controlled combustion process to produce a mixture of hydrogen and carbon monoxide. Auto-thermal reforming combines partial oxidation and steam reforming in a single process. Combined or two-step reforming involves multiple stages of gasification. Biomass gasification is a similar process, but it uses organic materials instead of coal as the feedstock.</p></p>
