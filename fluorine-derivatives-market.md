<p><p>Fluorine derivatives are substances that have fluorine atoms bonded to other elements or compounds. The market for fluorine derivatives can be categorized into various types. Monofluorides are compounds where fluorine is bonded with a single element. Hydrogen fluoride consists of one hydrogen atom and one fluorine atom. Xenon hexafluoroplatinate and xenon difluoride are compounds containing xenon and fluorine atoms. Tetrafluorides contain four fluorine atoms bonded with another element. Hexafluorides consist of six fluorine atoms bonded with another element. The "Others" category includes all other fluorine derivatives not mentioned above.</p></p>
