<p><p>Intraocular melanoma is a rare form of eye cancer that develops in the melanocytes, the pigment-producing cells within the eye. The treatment for intraocular melanoma depends on the size and location of the tumor, as well as the patient's overall health and preferences. The primary treatments for intraocular melanoma include radiation therapy, surgery, and occasionally, targeted therapy.</p><p>Radiation therapy, also known as brachytherapy, involves the use of radioactive materials to target and destroy cancer cells. Surgery may involve removing the tumor along with a small amount of healthy tissue surrounding it, or in some cases, the entire eye may need to be removed. Targeted therapy is a relatively new approach that uses drugs to specifically target and attack cancer cells.</p><p>As for the market growth analysis and latest trends in the intraocular melanoma treatment market, the industry is expected to witness a significant growth rate.
