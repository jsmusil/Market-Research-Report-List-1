<p><p>There are two main types of low voltage disconnect switch markets: fused and non-fused. In the fused market, the switch is integrated with a fuse, which helps protect the electrical system from overload and short circuits. Fused switches are commonly used in applications where higher levels of protection are required. On the other hand, non-fused switches do not have an integrated fuse and are typically used in applications where protection is not a top priority but reliable disconnection is needed. Both types serve the purpose of disconnecting circuits to prevent electrical damage.</p></p>
