<p><p>The Lane Keep Assist System (LKAS) is a driver-assistance technology that helps in keeping the vehicle within the lanes by automatically adjusting the steering wheel. It uses a camera or sensors to detect lane markings on the road and provides gentle steering corrections to prevent the vehicle from drifting out of the lane unintentionally. LKAS not only enhances safety but also reduces driver fatigue, as it minimizes the constant adjustments needed to stay in the lane.</p><p>Adaptive Cruise Control (ACC) is another advanced driver-assistance system that helps in maintaining a safe distance from the vehicle ahead. It uses sensors such as radar or lasers to detect the speed and distance of the vehicle in front and automatically adjusts the speed accordingly to maintain a safe following distance. ACC offers conveniences and safety benefits by reducing driver workload and the risk of rear-end collisions.</p><p>The Lane Keep Assist System and Adaptive Cruise Control market are witnessing significant growth due to the rising demand for advanced driver-assistance systems and increasing concerns regarding road safety. It assists the driver by automatically steering the vehicle to keep it within the lane. The Adaptive Cruise Control (ACC) system uses radar sensors to detect the distance between the vehicle and the one ahead. It maintains a safe distance by automatically adjusting the vehicle's speed. Both systems rely on an Electronic Control Unit (ECU) to process the sensor data and an EPAS Actuator to control the vehicle's steering.</p></p>
