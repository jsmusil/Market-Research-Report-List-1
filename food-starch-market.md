<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The food starch market can be categorized into four main types. Native starch is extracted directly from plants and is used as a thickening agent. Modified starch is chemically or physically treated to improve its functionality. Starch derivatives are further processed forms of starch, such as dextrin and maltodextrin, which have various applications in the food industry. Starch sweeteners, like glucose syrup and maltose, are produced by enzymatic hydrolysis of starch and are used as sweetening agents. Each type of starch serves a unique purpose and has different applications in the food industry.</p></p>
